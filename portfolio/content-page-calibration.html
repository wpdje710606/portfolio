Project: Calibration Service Page

To set up a webpage with multiple forms and content.

The company offers various services that require a landing page to showcase to customers. These services include Calibration, Repair, and Expert Chat. As a result, the page will need multiple forms, content with images, and a visually appealing layout to establish its presence. Let's review together what has been created here.

Layout and Nav

Sections

Navigation

Goal: To establish the appropriate hierarchy for each section to ensure a smooth flow of content.
Elements: Froms, Contents and Graphics
Process:

Several pieces of information need to be presented here - the types of calibration, repair services offered, and the expertise available. The page begins with the most crucial section - Calibration Service, which includes forms, certificates, and images. The page concludes with a Q&A section that customers can refer to as a final resource after reviewing the entire page.

Goal: Clear and informative indicators.
Elements: Click to scroll Navigation, Sidebar panel, and Anchor Indicators.
Process:

This detailed information page has a complex structure. To enhance user experience, I have included a sidebar panel and click-to-scroll functionality to facilitate easy navigation between sections. The sidebar panel displays section titles when hovered over and can be minimized if deemed unnecessary or obstructive. Additionally, a sticky header will appear for page navigation as the user scrolls.

Style and Functions

Style

Functions

Goal: Sleek and Organized.
Elements: Visual elements, buttons, and section separators.
Process:

The page will adhere to the company's sleek design standard across the entire website. As part of this, section dividers will be displayed between white and grey backgrounds, providing a subtle yet distinct way to separate each section. To enhance customer understanding, graphics such as lab images and actual working images have been included to visually represent the processes. Efforts have been made to ensure that the graphics blend seamlessly into the overall page design.

Goal: Easy to use
Elements: Forms display, Email setting and Direct Form URL
Process:

Multiple forms need to be displayed on the page, and instead of showing them directly, I have opted to present them as slide-in modals. This approach helps save space for customers who do not need to see the forms immediately. Each form allows customers to submit their requests, and an auto-reply email confirms that the request has been received. Additionally, the sales team has requested a URL that opens the calibration form when customers load the page. The page and forms will display slightly differently based on the customer's status - French, B2C, or B2B.

Mobile Design

Layout

Adjustment

Goal: To keep the same standard on Mobile Device.
Elements: NAV, Sections and Sticky Footer
Process:

Due to the limited screen size of mobile devices, the layout of sections has been adjusted from side-by-side to a linear format. The navigation will remain fixed at the top when scrolling, with only three main sections displayed directly and the rest accessible within a collapsible menu. Additionally, two buttons have been added to the sticky footer for easy access to the Calibration Form and contacting our experts, as these are key elements on the page.

Goal: Section Display
Elements: Graphic and Content
Process:

As the layout for mobile devices has been modified, I have also updated the appearance of each section to better suit the mobile layout. Graphics will be aligned with section headers, and content will be displayed directly below. Each section will have adequate spacing between them. The forms on the page will have their CSS adjusted to fit the mobile view, while the slide-in modals will remain unchanged.

Execution and Result

Execution

Result

Challange: Spacing and Multi Forms
Solution:

To ensure that all forms function properly with different content but maintain a consistent style, I collaborated with the development team to ensure a seamless user experience through thorough testing. Additionally, I focused on optimizing spacing to provide adequate division between sections while maintaining a smooth visual flow. By researching other websites and working closely with the manager, I ensured that our standards were followed and that the design was visually appealing and user-friendly.

Result:

The page has been successfully launched, and the feedback has been positive. It's a significant achievement that customer requests are now primarily coming through our website rather than the sales team. Moreover, customers are becoming more familiar with our services and what the company offers. Please take a moment to explore the page and feel free to provide feedback through the "Contact Me" page.
